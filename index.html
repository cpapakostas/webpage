Christos Papakostas

Postdoctoral Researcher (UniWA) • Research Associate (NKUA)

About

I am a scholar working at the intersection of Social Sciences, Humanities, and Information Technology, with a strong focus on the pedagogical, ethical, and societal implications of emerging digital technologies. I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Informatics and Computer Engineering at the University of West Attica. I completed my Ph.D. in the same department at the University of West Attica and I hold a Dipl.Eng. and an M.Sc. from the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Democritus University of Thrace. Additionally, I earned a B.Sc. degree in Social Theology and Religious Studies from the School of Theology at the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens, allowing me to approach complex humanistic questions through advanced computational and interdisciplinary methods.

My academic work examines how Artificial Intelligence, Augmented and Mixed Reality, and adaptive digital systems can support education, cultural understanding, and social engagement. Alongside my research activity, I bring extensive teaching, administrative, and professional experience in higher education, contributing to curriculum design, quality assurance, and digital transformation initiatives across diverse academic contexts.

My work is grounded in a human-centered perspective on technology, aiming to connect technological innovation with ethical reflection, social responsibility, and meaningful learning.

Research

Artificial Intelligence, Education, and Human-Centered Systems

This research strand focuses on the design and evaluation of AI-driven and data-informed systems for education and learning within broader social and humanistic contexts. My work explores pedagogical models, ethical considerations, and cognitive frameworks related to personalization, adaptivity, and user modeling in digital learning environments.

Special emphasis is placed on responsible and transparent AI, learner engagement, and the social impact of intelligent systems, contributing to the field of Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence and Educational Informatics.

Digital Technologies, Immersive Media, and Cultural & Social Applications

A second core research axis concerns the use of Augmented and Mixed Reality, immersive media, and interactive digital technologies in education, cultural contexts, and public-facing learning environments. My research investigates how spatial computing and experiential learning approaches can enhance understanding, participation, and accessibility in domains such as history, culture, and social learning.

This line of work positions digital technologies as tools for cultural interpretation, social inclusion, and interdisciplinary knowledge exchange, aligning with the broader field of Humanistic and Social Informatics.
